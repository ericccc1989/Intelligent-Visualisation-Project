How does the popularization of electric vehicles help China manage its CO2 emission?

China is well-known as the biggest country of CO2 emission in the past few decades. Electricity and heat generation, industrial activities, and transportation are some of the main reasons for that. However, in recent few years, the growth of CO2 emissions has been controlled effectively, especially in the transport sector. We cannot deny that this is somehow related to the popularisation of the electric vehicle in this Asian country.

Fighting Against CO2 Emission

Situation of CO2 Emission in China

When China entered the 21st century, urbanization vastly changed people's life quality but caused much more pollution at the same time. The construction, industrial, and transport activities here produce a large amount of CO2 and GHG, and this made China became the world's largest carbon footprint country in 2004. In 2016, China's CO2 emission amount was 9127.9 Mt, which is 29% of the world's total. In comparison, the second-largest CO2 emission country the USA contributes 14%, and the third-largest country, India contributes 7% of the world total. 

Construction-related activities in China help its infrastructure in the transport sector grow fastly. Private vehicles, public transport, and transit vehicles support people's daily life and work here. A large population base means that the CO2 emission in the transport sector also can not be a small number. If we look at how each sector contributes to China's CO2 emission, besides electricity and heat generation and industrial activities, transportation is the third-largest sector of CO2 emission.

Although dealing with CO2 emission in China seems like an impossible mission, we can still find the growth rate of total CO2 emission in the recent few years is much slower than before. The year change rate had even come to a negative number of -0.3% for the first time(which means emission is decreasing). Is it related to the electric vehicle? What about the EV market in these years?

The EV Revolution

How did the EVs Market in China grow rapidly?

In 2010, there were only 17,000 electric vehicles on the roads in the whole world. China wasn't a leading country on EVs stock at that time. The EVs stock in China was 32.2 thousand vehicles in 2013, and this is a much smaller number compared with the USA and Japan. However, it grows rapidly to 334.9 million vehicles in 2019, which is 47% of the world's total stock. The electric vehicle is starting to replace traditional ICE vehicles, and China may be the first country to enter the electric transport era.

The Chinese government also has a clear plan for its future EV development. In the stated policy scenario released by IEA, China is going to have a much more systematic industry in 2030. The electric LDV will possess 40% of the total stock, and the electric bus will possess 60% of the total stock at that time. Surprisingly, all the two-wheel vehicles will be electric vehicles by 2030 in China.

Reasons behind the rise of EVs market in China:

CO2&GHG Emission:

A large population base in China is the main reason for high CO2&GHG emissions here. This country's economy and citizens' life is relying on transportation and mobility. Traditional ICE cars inevitably produce CO2 and other greenhouse gases. However, high pollution and emission are not appropriate for a country's long-term sustainable development. Electric vehicles have become a natural solution for ICE's replacement.

Gasoline Price:

The crude oil source in China relies heavily on other foreign countries such as Saudi Arabia and Russia. This made the Asian country become the world's largest crude oil imports country. In addition, the oil tax is an important part of the government's income. Thus, the gasoline price in China gradually becomes unaffordable for some families. This was even worse when the covid-19 pandemic outbreak happened in 2020. For a large number of people, an electric vehicle without oil costs seems like a better choice for them. 

Ride-Hailing Service:

The ride-hailing service market in China is very common and advanced, and traditional taxi service is gradually being replaced by it. Most citizens like it because of its low cost and convenience. Using the electric vehicle for ride-hailing service is already popularized, and drivers and companies are all willing to use it due to its low budget and safety. Some EV manufacturers, such as Geely Auto, also release their own ride-hailing services. 

Government Policy:

In most of the major cities in China, traffic restriction is really common. The government uses this policy to release the traffic pressure in the city. Blue license plate(traditional ICE car) owners cannot drive their car on the road in one day each week. However, green license plate (EV car) owners are not limited by this policy. Besides this, the welfare of buying an electric vehicle is much better than buying a traditional ICE car. Those policies attract a large number of customers.

The change

Does Electric Vehicles Really Effective?

All the facts are supported by real data. If we take a look at China's EVs stock column graph, the number of electric vehicles has an obvious growth each year. For the total CO2 emissions, it is hard to directly tell the change number each year directly from a line graph. That is why we also need to generate an additional change value from it. Yearly change is the emission value this year minus the emission value in the previous year. If we made yearly change value into a line graph and compare it with EVs stock, it is clear that with the increase of EVs stock in China, the yearly change of CO2 emissions is reducing. In other words, electric vehicles are helping China controls its CO2 emissions.

The Future

Due to the condition in China, the EVs have already been popularized in this Asian country, and China has become the leading country in the EVs market for many years. For the purpose of sustainable development, the electric vehicle is the best solution for controlling CO2 emissions in the transportation sector. For a short time, there is still a large space for the electric vehicle market to grow due to its technique shortcoming. However, all the countries should pay attention to the potential of electric vehicles. One day the technology of electric vehicles will be mature and comprehensive enough that it can replace ICE vehicles completely. China will also be playing an important role in it.
